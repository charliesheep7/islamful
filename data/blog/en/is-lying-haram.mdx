## The Ruling

**Lying is haram in Islam.** The prohibition is clear, direct, and agreed upon by scholars of every major school of thought. Truthfulness (_sidq_) is one of the highest virtues in Islam, and its opposite — lying (_kadhib_) — is one of the most despised sins. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) explicitly warned that lying leads a person to wickedness and ultimately to the Hellfire.

> **Quick Answer:** Lying is **haram** (forbidden) in Islam. The Quran and Sunnah leave no room for doubt. There are only **three narrow exceptions** from the hadith. Outside of those, a Muslim must speak the truth or remain silent.

## The Evidence

The Quran and Sunnah contain some of the strongest language in all of Islamic scripture when it comes to condemning dishonesty.

### From the Quran

Allah says in [Surah Al-Hajj, 22:30](https://quran.com/22/30):

> فَاجْتَنِبُوا الرِّجْسَ مِنَ الْأَوْثَانِ وَاجْتَنِبُوا قَوْلَ الزُّورِ

> _Fajtanibu al-rijsa min al-awthani wajtanibu qawla al-zur_

> "So avoid the uncleanliness of idols and avoid false statement."

Notice how Allah places **lying directly alongside idol worship** in the same verse. This pairing shows just how gravely Islam treats dishonesty.

Allah also says in [Surah Az-Zumar, 39:3](https://quran.com/39/3):

> إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَهْدِي مَنْ هُوَ كَاذِبٌ كَفَّارٌ

> _Inna Allaha la yahdi man huwa kadhibun kaffar_

> "Indeed, Allah does not guide one who is a liar and [confirmed] disbeliever."

And in [Surah Al-Mu'minun, 23:1-3](https://quran.com/23/1-3), Allah describes the successful believers as those who turn away from idle and false speech. Guarding one's tongue from lies is just as important as guarding it from [cursing](/blog/is-cursing-haram) and other harmful speech.

### From the Hadith

The most important hadith on this topic is the famous narration in which the Prophet (peace be upon him) said:

> إِنَّ الصِّدْقَ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْبِرِّ وَإِنَّ الْبِرَّ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ وَإِنَّ الرَّجُلَ لَيَصْدُقُ حَتَّى يُكْتَبَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ صِدِّيقًا وَإِنَّ الْكَذِبَ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْفُجُورِ وَإِنَّ الْفُجُورَ يَهْدِي إِلَى النَّارِ وَإِنَّ الرَّجُلَ لَيَكْذِبُ حَتَّى يُكْتَبَ عِنْدَ اللَّهِ كَذَّابًا

> _"Indeed, truthfulness leads to righteousness and righteousness leads to Paradise. A man continues to be truthful until he is recorded with Allah as a truthful person (siddiq). And indeed, lying leads to wickedness and wickedness leads to the Hellfire. A man continues to lie until he is recorded with Allah as a liar."_

> [Narrated by Al-Bukhari, 6094 and Muslim, 2607](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:6094)

This hadith lays out the spiritual trajectory of both the truthful person and the liar in the clearest possible terms.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) also identified lying as a hallmark of hypocrisy:

> آيَةُ الْمُنَافِقِ ثَلَاثٌ إِذَا حَدَّثَ كَذَبَ وَإِذَا وَعَدَ أَخْلَفَ وَإِذَا اؤْتُمِنَ خَانَ

> _"The signs of the hypocrite are three: when he speaks, he lies; when he makes a promise, he breaks it; and when he is entrusted, he betrays."_

> [Narrated by Al-Bukhari, 33 and Muslim, 59](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:33)

## Scholar Opinions

**The scholars of all four madhabs unanimously agree that lying is haram.** This is one of the most well-established rulings in Islamic jurisprudence, with virtually no disagreement.

**Imam Al-Nawawi** (Shafi'i school) discussed lying extensively in _Riyad al-Salihin_, dedicating a full chapter to the prohibition of falsehood. He stated that lying is haram by the consensus of the Muslim scholars, and that it is among the most dangerous sins of the tongue.

**Ibn al-Qayyim** (Hanbali school) wrote in _Al-Jawab al-Kafi_ that lying corrupts the heart, darkens the soul, and distances a person from Allah. He described it as the root of many other sins, because a person who lies will eventually find it easier to commit other transgressions.

**Imam Al-Qurtubi** (Maliki school) noted in his tafsir that Allah's pairing of lying with idolatry in Surah Al-Hajj demonstrates the extreme severity of the sin. He held that habitual lying can even endanger a person's faith.

**Ibn Abidin** (Hanafi school) classified lying as categorically haram and included it under the broader framework of sins that harm both the individual and the community.

| School  | Ruling on Lying | Notes                                   |
| ------- | --------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Hanafi  | **Haram**       | Harmful to the individual and community |
| Maliki  | **Haram**       | Paired with shirk in severity           |
| Shafi'i | **Haram**       | Consensus of scholars; major sin        |
| Hanbali | **Haram**       | Root of corruption in the heart         |

## Conditions and Gray Areas

While the general prohibition is absolute, the Sunnah identifies **three specific exceptions** where a form of lying is permitted. Umm Kulthum bint Uqbah (may Allah be pleased with her) reported that the Prophet (peace be upon him) said:

> لَيْسَ الْكَذَّابُ الَّذِي يُصْلِحُ بَيْنَ النَّاسِ فَيَنْمِي خَيْرًا أَوْ يَقُولُ خَيْرًا

> _"He is not a liar who reconciles between people, conveying good or saying good."_

> [Narrated by Muslim, 2605](https://sunnah.com/muslim:2605)

The narration goes on to identify **three cases**:

1. **Lying during wartime** — military deception and strategy to protect the Muslim community.
2. **Lying to reconcile between people** — telling each party that the other spoke well of them, to mend a broken relationship.
3. **A husband or wife speaking kindly to each other** — exaggerating affection or compliments to maintain marital harmony.

**These exceptions are narrow.** Scholars emphasize that even in these cases, using indirect speech (_tawriyah_) — saying something technically true but understood differently — is preferred over outright lying. The principle is to achieve the good objective with the least amount of deception possible.

**Exaggeration in speech** is also treated as a form of lying. The Prophet (peace be upon him) warned against making claims one cannot back up and against exaggerating in order to impress people. Similarly, **breaking a promise** you fully intended to keep is not lying, but making a promise you never intended to fulfill is a form of dishonesty and one of the signs of hypocrisy mentioned above.

## Common Questions

### Are white lies haram?

**Generally, yes.** Islam does not carve out a special category for "small" lies. The standard the Prophet (peace be upon him) set is clear: speak the truth or remain silent. If a white lie falls under one of the three exceptions — such as sparing your spouse's feelings or reconciling between friends — it may be permitted. Otherwise, it is still sinful, even if the harm seems minor. Small lies erode trust and can become habitual, which is exactly the trajectory the hadith warns about.

### Is exaggeration considered lying?

**Yes, when it distorts the truth.** Scholars distinguish between rhetorical emphasis that everyone understands as figurative and deliberate exaggeration meant to deceive. If you overstate something to mislead another person — about a product you are selling, your qualifications, or an event that happened — that is a form of lying. This is relevant in areas like [trading](/blog/is-trading-haram) and business, where honesty is an obligation.

### What if I lied? How do I repent?

Repentance from lying requires three things: **sincerely regretting** the lie, **stopping immediately**, and **resolving not to repeat it**. If your lie harmed someone else — for instance, if it damaged their reputation or caused them a financial loss — you must also make it right with them. The door of repentance is always open, and Allah is the Most Forgiving. Staying consistent with your [daily prayers](/prayer-times) helps strengthen your resolve to remain truthful. Making [dua for anxiety](/blog/dua-for-anxiety) can also help, since fear and stress are common reasons people resort to dishonesty.

## Summary

**Lying is unambiguously haram in Islam.** The Quran places it alongside the gravest of sins, and the Prophet (peace be upon him) warned that it leads to wickedness and the Hellfire. All four schools of Islamic law agree without exception. The only three permitted cases are wartime strategy, reconciling between people, and maintaining spousal harmony — and even then, scholars recommend indirect speech over outright lies. Guard your tongue, be truthful in all your dealings, and remember that every word you speak is being recorded. Truthfulness is not just a rule; it is a path that leads to righteousness and, ultimately, to Paradise.

And Allah knows best (والله أعلم).
