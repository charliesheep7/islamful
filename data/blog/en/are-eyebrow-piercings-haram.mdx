**Eyebrow piercings are considered haram by the majority of Islamic scholars.** The two primary reasons are: unnecessary bodily harm and imitating non-Muslim fashion practices (_tashabbuh_) without Islamic precedent.

> **Quick Answer:** Eyebrow piercings are generally haram in Islam. Unlike ear piercings for women — which have clear Sunnah basis — eyebrow piercings have no Islamic precedent, involve unnecessary harm to the face, and are associated with imitating specific non-Muslim fashion trends.

## The Evidence

### Prohibition of Unnecessary Harm

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) established a fundamental principle:

**Arabic:**

> لَا ضَرَرَ وَلَا ضِرَارَ

**Transliteration:**

> _Lā ḍarara wa lā ḍirār_

**Translation:**

> "There should be no harm and no reciprocating harm." (Narrated by Ibn Majah, 2341; classified as sahih)

Eyebrow piercings involve puncturing the face with a needle, creating an open wound that carries risk of infection, nerve damage, and scarring. Without a valid justification, this falls under causing unnecessary harm to the body that Islam seeks to protect.

### The Prohibition of Tashabbuh

The Prophet ﷺ said:

**Arabic:**

> مَنْ تَشَبَّهَ بِقَوْمٍ فَهُوَ مِنْهُمْ

**Transliteration:**

> _Man tashabbaha biqawmin fahuwa minhum_

**Translation:**

> "Whoever imitates a people is one of them." (Narrated by Abu Dawud, 4031 — graded as hasan sahih)

Eyebrow piercings entered mainstream culture as a specifically counter-cultural and non-Muslim fashion practice. Scholars use this hadith to argue that intentionally adopting such practices without an Islamic basis falls under prohibited imitation.

## Scholar Opinions

| Scholar/Position                         | Ruling                     | Reasoning                                |
| ---------------------------------------- | -------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Majority of contemporary scholars        | Haram                      | Unnecessary harm + tashabbuh             |
| Permanent Fatwa Committee (Saudi Arabia) | Haram                      | No Islamic basis, tashabbuh              |
| Dar al-Ifta al-Misriyyah (Egypt)         | Strongly disliked to haram | Unnecessary harm to the face             |
| Some Hanafi scholars                     | Makruh (strongly disliked) | Less emphasis on tashabbuh, more on harm |

The mainstream scholarly position is that eyebrow piercings are haram. A minority considers them makruh (strongly disliked) but not strictly forbidden. Very few scholars permit them.

## Conditions and Gray Areas

**For women vs. men** — Women in Islam have broader permission for adornment than men. However, even for women, the permissible adornments recognized in Islamic law are specific — ears, nose (in applicable cultural contexts), and the like. The eyebrow does not fall into established categories of female adornment in fiqh literature.

**If you already have an eyebrow piercing** — Most scholars advise removing it. The longer it is kept, the more normalized the practice becomes for the individual. If removal would cause significant medical complications (unlikely in most cases), wait until it is safe.

**Temporary adornment vs. permanent piercing** — There is a distinction between wearing a clip-on or adhesive brow ring (no harm to the body) and actual piercing. A clip-on accessory does not involve bodily harm. However, it may still raise concerns about tashabbuh depending on intent and display context.

**Microblading / Eyebrow tattoos** — This is a separate, more debated question. Many scholars permit permanent eyebrow makeup (tattooing for cosmetic purposes) for women when it is done to correct sparse brows, distinguishing it from the generally prohibited tattooing in Islam.

## Common Questions

**Q: Ear piercings are allowed — why not eyebrow piercings?**
Ear piercings for women have direct Sunnah evidence. When the Prophet ﷺ encouraged women to give charity, they donated their ear jewelry (Narrated by Bukhari, 1466), affirming that ear piercings are an established, recognized practice in Islamic tradition. Eyebrow piercings have no equivalent basis and are not mentioned in any classical fiqh text as permissible adornment.

**Q: What if I got the piercing before becoming Muslim or learning about this ruling?**
You are not sinful for what you did in ignorance. Most scholars advise removing it going forward, but there is no sin for the past action. Focus on making the decision with the knowledge you now have.

**Q: Can a Muslim doctor pierce eyebrows for medical reasons?**
If there is a genuine medical reason that requires a piercing in or near the eyebrow (such as treatment for a specific condition), necessity (_darura_) would apply, and the ruling changes. This is an extremely rare scenario.

## Summary

**Eyebrow piercings are haram according to the majority of scholars.**

**Key points:**

- Unnecessary bodily harm is prohibited in Islam
- Tashabbuh (imitating non-Muslim practices without basis) is prohibited
- Ear piercings for women have Sunnah evidence; eyebrow piercings do not
- If you already have one, scholars advise removing it
- The ruling applies to both men and women, though slightly differently
