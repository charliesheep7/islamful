Death is a reality every soul must face. When a loved one passes, Islam gives us a powerful and merciful gift: the ability to continue helping them through sincere supplication. These are the most authentic duas for the deceased, drawn directly from the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ.

<DuaCollection lang="en" />

## The Core Dua for the Deceased

This is the essential supplication taught by the Prophet ﷺ for anyone who has died. It covers the four greatest gifts you can ask Allah to bestow: forgiveness, mercy, safety, and pardon.

**Arabic:**

> اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لَهُ وَارْحَمْهُ وَعَافِهِ وَاعْفُ عَنْهُ

**Transliteration:**

> _Allāhumma ighfir lahu warḥamhu wa ʿāfihi waʿfu ʿanhu_

**Translation:**

> "O Allah, forgive him, have mercy on him, grant him safety, and pardon him."

**Source:** Narrated by Muslim, 963

For a deceased woman, replace _lahu_ (him) with _lahā_ (her): **Allāhumma ighfir lahā warḥamhā wa ʿāfihā waʿfu ʿanhā.**

## The Full Janazah Dua

This is the comprehensive supplication recited during the janazah (funeral) prayer. It covers the living and the dead alike.

**Arabic:**

> اللَّهُمَّ اغْفِرْ لِحَيِّنَا وَمَيِّتِنَا وَشَاهِدِنَا وَغَائِبِنَا وَصَغِيرِنَا وَكَبِيرِنَا وَذَكَرِنَا وَأُنْثَانَا اللَّهُمَّ مَنْ أَحْيَيْتَهُ مِنَّا فَأَحْيِهِ عَلَى الْإِسْلَامِ وَمَنْ تَوَفَّيْتَهُ مِنَّا فَتَوَفَّهُ عَلَى الْإِيمَانِ

**Transliteration:**

> _Allāhumma ighfir liḥayyinā wa mayyitinā wa shāhidinā wa ghāʾibinā wa ṣaghīrinā wa kabīrinā wa dhakarinā wa unthānā. Allāhumma man aḥyaytahu minnā fa-aḥyihi ʿalal-islām, wa man tawaffaytahu minnā fa-tawaffahu ʿalal-īmān_

**Translation:**

> "O Allah, forgive our living and our dead, those present and those absent, our young and our old, our males and our females. O Allah, whomever You give life among us, let him live upon Islam, and whomever You take in death among us, let him die upon faith."

**Source:** Abu Dawud, 3201; Tirmidhi, 1024

This dua is recited in the third takbir of the janazah prayer. Memorising it is one of the most valuable acts a Muslim can do — none of us knows when we will need to lead or participate in a funeral prayer.

## Dua When Visiting Graves

When you visit a Muslim graveyard, the Prophet ﷺ taught us to greet its inhabitants and pray for them.

**Arabic:**

> السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ أَهْلَ الدِّيَارِ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَالْمُسْلِمِينَ وَإِنَّا إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ بِكُمْ لَلَاحِقُونَ نَسْأَلُ اللَّهَ لَنَا وَلَكُمُ الْعَافِيَةَ

**Transliteration:**

> _Assalāmu ʿalaykum ahl al-diyāri minal-muʾminīna wal-muslimīn, wa innā in shāʾa Allāhu bikum lalāḥiqūn, nasʾalullāha lanā wa lakumul-ʿāfiyah_

**Translation:**

> "Peace be upon you, O people of these dwellings, from among the believers and the Muslims. Indeed, we will, Allah willing, join you. We ask Allah for well-being for us and for you."

**Source:** Muslim, 975

## Dua for Deceased Parents

The Quran itself contains a dua specifically for parents — including after their passing.

**Arabic:**

> رَّبِّ ارْحَمْهُمَا كَمَا رَبَّيَانِي صَغِيرًا

**Transliteration:**

> _Rabbir ḥamhumā kamā rabbayānī ṣaghīrā_

**Translation:**

> "My Lord, have mercy on them both as they raised me when I was young."

**Source:** Surah Al-Isra, 17:24

Recite this regularly for your parents — alive or deceased. The Prophet ﷺ confirmed that a righteous child's dua reaches the deceased parent: "When a person dies, his deeds come to an end except for three: ongoing charity, knowledge that is benefited from, and a righteous child who prays for him." (Muslim, 1631)

You can find this and more supplications in our complete [Dua Collection](/dua).

## When to Recite These Duas

- **Upon hearing of a death** — say _Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un_ (Al-Baqarah 2:156) then follow with the core dua
- **At the janazah prayer** — recite the full janazah dua in the third takbir
- **When visiting graves** — begin with the greeting for the deceased, then make dua
- **After every salah** — a few moments of dua for your deceased loved ones is one of the greatest gifts you can offer them
- **Any time** — there is no restriction on when you may supplicate for the dead

Check your [prayer times](/prayer-times) to build a consistent habit of making dua after each of your five daily prayers.

## The Mercy of Allah for the Deceased

Islam teaches that the living and the dead remain connected through supplication and righteous deeds. Every dua you make for a deceased Muslim is an act of worship rewarded by Allah ﷻ — for you and, by His mercy, for them.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "The dua of a Muslim for his brother in his absence is answered." (Muslim, 2733). This applies equally to those who have already passed. Do not underestimate the power of sincere supplication — it may be the means by which Allah ﷻ elevates the rank of your loved one in the grave and in the Hereafter.
