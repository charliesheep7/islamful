Learning to pray salah can feel overwhelming at first — the Arabic, the postures, the different counts for each prayer. But here is the reassuring part: every one of the five daily prayers follows the same simple pattern. Once you can pray two rakahs, you can pray all five. The only thing that changes is how many times you repeat that pattern.

Salah is the second pillar of Islam and the first thing you will be asked about on the Day of Judgment. Allah (SWT) made it obligatory at fixed times: "Indeed, prayer has been decreed upon the believers a decree of specified times" ([Surah An-Nisa, 4:103](https://quran.com/4/103)). This guide walks you through it from zero — no prior knowledge assumed.

**What you'll learn:**

- The five daily prayers and how many rakahs each one has
- What you need to do before you pray
- The full step-by-step method, with what to say at each posture
- The differences between the madhabs and the mistakes beginners make most

## What Is Salah and Why Does It Matter?

Salah (صَلَاة) is the formal Islamic prayer performed five times a day at set times. It is not a free-form conversation with God — it is a fixed sequence of standing, bowing, and prostrating, with specific words, taught exactly by the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

That fixed form is intentional. When the Prophet ﷺ taught his companions, he said plainly:

> صَلُّوا كَمَا رَأَيْتُمُونِي أُصَلِّي

> _Sallu kama ra'aytumuni usalli_

> "Pray as you have seen me praying."
> — [Sahih al-Bukhari 631](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:631)

So your goal as a beginner is not to invent anything. It is to copy the Prophet's prayer as closely as you can. Allah pairs prayer with success throughout the Quran and commands believers to "guard strictly the prayers, especially the middle prayer, and stand before Allah devoutly obedient" ([Surah Al-Baqarah, 2:238](https://quran.com/2/238)).

## The Five Daily Prayers

Before the postures, learn the schedule. Each prayer has a window of time and a number of obligatory (fard) rakahs — a rakah being one full cycle of standing, bowing, and two prostrations.

| Prayer | Time | Fard Rakahs |
|---|---|---|
| Fajr | Dawn, before sunrise | 2 |
| Dhuhr | After midday | 4 |
| Asr | Late afternoon | 4 |
| Maghrib | Just after sunset | 3 |
| Isha | Night | 4 |

Memorize this table first. Each prayer also has recommended **sunnah** rakahs you can add for extra reward, but the fard counts above are the non-negotiable core. The exact clock times shift daily with the sun, so check your local [prayer times](/prayer-times) rather than guessing.

## Before You Begin

Three things must be in place before you start, or the prayer is not valid:

- **Wudu (ablution).** You must be in a state of ritual purity. If you are not sure how, follow our full guide on [how to perform wudu](/blog/how-to-perform-wudu) first — it takes about a minute.
- **A clean place and clothing.** Your body, clothes, and the spot you pray on must be free of impurity. Cover the awrah: for men, navel to knee at minimum; for women, everything except the face and hands.
- **Face the qiblah.** Turn toward the Kaaba in Makkah. Most phones have a qiblah compass, and almost every mosque marks the direction.

Then make your **intention (niyyah)**. This is in the heart — simply intend which prayer you are about to perform. You do not say it aloud.

## Step-by-Step: How to Pray Two Rakahs

We will learn Fajr first because it is only two rakahs, making it the simplest complete prayer. Every other prayer is built from these same units.

1. **Make the opening takbir.** Stand upright, raise your hands to your ears or shoulders, and say:

   > اللَّهُ أَكْبَر — _Allahu Akbar_ — "Allah is the Greatest."

   This enters you into the prayer. From now until the salam, stay focused — no talking or turning away.

2. **Recite the opening, then Al-Fatiha.** Place your right hand over your left on your chest. Many begin with a short opening dua, then recite Surah Al-Fatiha — the one chapter required in every single rakah:

   The Prophet ﷺ said there is no prayer for the one who does not recite [Surah Al-Fatiha](https://quran.com/1) ([Sahih al-Bukhari 756](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:756)). After it, recite any other short surah or a few verses you know.

3. **Bow (ruku).** Say _Allahu Akbar_, bend at the waist with your back flat and hands on your knees, and say three times:

   > سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيم — _Subhana Rabbi al-Adheem_ — "Glory to my Lord, the Most Great."

4. **Rise from bowing.** Stand straight again and say:

   > سَمِعَ اللَّهُ لِمَنْ حَمِدَهُ، رَبَّنَا وَلَكَ الْحَمْد — _Sami'a Allahu liman hamidah, Rabbana wa laka al-hamd_ — "Allah hears the one who praises Him. Our Lord, to You is all praise."

5. **Prostrate (sujud).** Say _Allahu Akbar_ and go down so your forehead, nose, both palms, both knees, and toes all touch the ground. Say three times:

   > سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَى — _Subhana Rabbi al-A'la_ — "Glory to my Lord, the Most High."

6. **Sit briefly, then prostrate again.** Say _Allahu Akbar_, sit up calmly for a moment, then say _Allahu Akbar_ and make the second prostration exactly like the first. That completes **one rakah**.

7. **Stand and pray the second rakah.** Rise saying _Allahu Akbar_ and repeat steps 2–6: Al-Fatiha, another short surah, bowing, and two prostrations.

8. **Sit for the final tashahhud.** After the second prostration of the last rakah, sit and recite the tashahhud, then send blessings on the Prophet ﷺ:

   > التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلَّهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ، السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ

   > _At-tahiyyatu lillahi was-salawatu wat-tayyibat, as-salamu alayka ayyuha an-nabiyyu wa rahmatullahi wa barakatuh_

   > "All greetings, prayers, and good things are for Allah. Peace be upon you, O Prophet, and the mercy of Allah and His blessings."

9. **End with the salam.** Turn your face to the right and say, then to the left and repeat:

   > السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّه — _As-salamu alaykum wa rahmatullah_ — "Peace and the mercy of Allah be upon you."

That is one complete prayer. For Maghrib (3 rakahs) and the four-rakah prayers, you simply add more rakahs before the final sitting — sitting briefly for the tashahhud after the second rakah, then standing to finish the rest.

## How Many Rakahs With Sunnah Prayers?

Once the fard prayers feel natural, you can add the regular sunnah rakahs the Prophet ﷺ prayed alongside them. These are optional but highly rewarded.

| Prayer | Sunnah before | Fard | Sunnah after |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fajr | 2 | 2 | — |
| Dhuhr | 2 or 4 | 4 | 2 |
| Asr | — | 4 | — |
| Maghrib | — | 3 | 2 |
| Isha | — | 4 | 2 |

Do not feel pressured to add all of these on day one. Master the fard first; the sunnah comes naturally once the core is solid.

## Differences Between the Madhabs

All four schools of thought agree on the essential structure above — the takbir, Al-Fatiha, bowing, prostrating, and salam are universal. They differ only on small details of posture and wording.

| Aspect | Hanafi | Shafi'i | Maliki | Hanbali |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hand position when standing | Below the navel | On the chest | Hands at the sides | Below the navel |
| Saying "Bismillah" aloud | Silently | Aloud in loud prayers | Omitted | Silently |
| Raising hands (after the first takbir) | Only at the start | At several points | Only at the start | At several points |

None of these makes another school's prayer invalid. If you learn from a local teacher or mosque, simply follow the way they teach — it will be one of these valid positions.

## Common Mistakes Beginners Make

- **Praying too fast.** Each posture needs stillness (tuma'ninah). The Prophet ﷺ once told a man who rushed his prayer to go back and pray again, because he had not settled in each position ([Sahih al-Bukhari 757](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:757)). Pause and let your body rest in every bow and prostration.
- **Skipping Al-Fatiha.** It is required in every rakah, not just the first. Without it, that rakah does not count.
- **Forgetting to face the qiblah.** Always confirm the direction before the opening takbir.
- **Restarting over small doubts.** If you lose count, build on the lower number you are sure of and add the two prostrations of forgetfulness (sujud as-sahw) at the end. Do not start the whole prayer over.
- **Lifting the forehead too soon.** Your nose and forehead must both rest on the ground in prostration, and you must be still before rising.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I read salah from my phone while learning?**
Yes — while you are still memorizing, glancing at the words on a phone or card is fine and better than abandoning the prayer. Aim to memorize Al-Fatiha and the core phrases as soon as you can, since they repeat in every rakah.

**What if I miss a prayer?**
Pray it as soon as you remember. The Prophet ﷺ taught that whoever forgets a prayer should pray it when they recall it. Make it up rather than skipping it entirely.

**How long does it take to learn?**
Most beginners can pray a full prayer confidently within two to four weeks of daily practice. The repetition does the work — by the fifth prayer of the day, the sequence is already becoming muscle memory.

## Summary

Salah looks complex from the outside, but it is one repeating pattern: stand and recite, bow, prostrate twice, and after the last rakah, sit and give salam.

- Learn the **two-rakah Fajr** first, then add rakahs for the longer prayers.
- The fard counts are **2-4-4-3-4** for Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha.
- Recite **Al-Fatiha in every rakah** and move with stillness, copying the Prophet's prayer ([Sahih al-Bukhari 631](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:631)).

Start today with one prayer, and let the habit grow. And Allah knows best (والله أعلم).
