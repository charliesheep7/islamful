Travel is one of the times when duas are especially answered. The Prophet ﷺ established a specific supplication for every stage of the journey — from the moment you leave your door to the moment you return home. Here is the complete collection.

## Dua When Leaving Home

**Arabic:**

> بِسْمِ اللَّهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ

**Transliteration:**

> _Bismillāhi tawakkaltu ʿalallāhi lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāh_

**Translation:**

> "In the name of Allah, I place my trust in Allah, and there is no might or power except with Allah."

**Source:** Abu Dawud 5095, Tirmidhi 3426 — graded as sahih

The Prophet ﷺ said that when a person recites this at their door, an angel declares: "You are guided, defended, and protected." Satan withdraws from such a person. Begin every journey — even a short errand — with these words.

## Dua When Boarding a Vehicle or Beginning the Journey

**Arabic:**

> سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي سَخَّرَ لَنَا هَذَا وَمَا كُنَّا لَهُ مُقْرِنِينَ وَإِنَّا إِلَى رَبِّنَا لَمُنقَلِبُونَ

**Transliteration:**

> _Subḥānal-ladhī sakhkhara lanā hādhā wa mā kunnā lahū muqrinīn, wa innā ilā Rabbinā lamunqalibūn_

**Translation:**

> "Glory be to the One Who has subjected this to us, and we were not capable of it ourselves. And indeed, we will return to our Lord."

**Source:** Surah Az-Zukhruf 43:13-14; Muslim 1342

Recite this when you step into a car, board a plane, mount an animal, or begin any mode of transport. The phrase _wa innā ilā Rabbinā lamunqalibūn_ — "we will return to our Lord" — is a profound reminder that every departure is a rehearsal for the final journey.

## Dua Upon Entering a New Town or City

**Arabic:**

> اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ خَيْرَهَا وَخَيْرَ أَهْلِهَا وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّهَا وَشَرِّ أَهْلِهَا

**Transliteration:**

> _Allāhumma innī asʾaluka khayraha wa khayra ahlihā wa aʿūdhu bika min sharrihā wa sharri ahlihā_

**Translation:**

> "O Allah, I ask You for its good and the good of its people, and I seek refuge in You from its evil and the evil of its people."

**Source:** Ibn Majah 3887 — graded as hasan

Say this when arriving at any unfamiliar destination. Whether you are entering a new city for work, visiting family, or arriving for Hajj or Umrah, this dua puts your arrival under Allah's protection.

## Dua When Returning Home from Travel

**Arabic:**

> آيِبُونَ تَائِبُونَ عَابِدُونَ لِرَبِّنَا حَامِدُونَ

**Transliteration:**

> _Āyibūna tāʾibūna ʿābidūna li-Rabbinā ḥāmidūn_

**Translation:**

> "We return, repenting, worshipping, and praising our Lord."

**Source:** Bukhari 3084, Muslim 1342

The Prophet ﷺ would say _Allahu Akbar_ three times upon seeing his city on the horizon, then recite this dua. It is also sunnah to pray two voluntary rak'ahs upon returning home — a prayer of gratitude for a safe return.

## The Full Sequence

Follow this order for a complete journey:

1. **At your door:** Dua for leaving home
2. **Boarding transport:** Dua for mounting a vehicle, then add: _Allahumma hawin 'alayna safarana hadha_ ("O Allah, make this journey easy for us")
3. **Arriving at destination:** Dua for entering a new town
4. **Returning home:** Three takbirs + dua for returning, then pray two rak'ahs

## Why Travel Duas Matter

The traveller is in a state of vulnerability and dependence — far from home, relying on means beyond their full control. This is precisely why the Prophet ﷺ established a dua for every transition of the journey. Each supplication is an act of tawakkul (reliance on Allah) and a reminder that safety comes from Him alone, not from the plane, the road, or the driver.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "A traveller's dua is not rejected." (Tirmidhi 3598) Use every stage of your journey as an opportunity for supplication — for yourself, your family, and all Muslims.
